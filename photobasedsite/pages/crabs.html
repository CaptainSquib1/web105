Crab

Crabs exhibit a remarkable diversity of adaptations. Some crabs, like the coconut crab, are terrestrial and can climb trees to retrieve coconuts. Others, like the decorator crab, adorn their shells with bits of seaweed and debris as camouflage. Crabs also have specialized behaviors for reproduction. For example, male fiddler crabs use their large claws to perform elaborate courtship displays to attract females, showcasing the fascinating variety within this group of crustaceans.
